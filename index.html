Data Analysis for Business, Economics and Policy


Data Analysis for Business, Economics and Policy is a textbook that introduces students to the many steps data analysis, provides an integrated knowledge of the most important tools for exploratory, predictive, or causal analysis, and prepares students to carry out data analysis on their own, using real life data that is often messy and not well structured. The textbook includes 47 fully developed case studies using 27 datasets from all over the world covering diverse topics. We share the raw data, the clean data files, and all codes for cleaning and analysis in both Stata and R (working on Python).

The textbook can support a year-long course of introducing data analysis in professional or MA programs, and its parts can be used to support shorter courses. It has 24 chapters, structured into four parts:

I Data Exploration
II Regression Analysis
III Prediction
IV Causal Analysis
